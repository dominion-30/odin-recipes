Ewedu and Gbegiri

Visit Home for more recipes

Description

This meal originates from Western Africa and particulary from the Yoruba people in South Western Nigeria

The Gbegiri is made from beans by boiling and later using the mixture left, while the Ewedu is prepared by boiling the Ewedu leaf and grounding it with a specilized clean broom. The Stew is prepared by boiling tomatoe and ground pepper to a desired mixture after adding meat stock and seasoning. What the soup is eated with, the Amala is gotten by putting yam or cassava flour in boiling water and stiring to get the desired consistency.

Ingredients

  • Beans
  • Ewedu leaf
  • Tomatoes
  • Pepper
  • Onion
  • Protein Source

Steps

It is not cast in stone that you must follow this steps in accendding or incremental order. For example you are free to make the Stew first

  1. Make the Gbegiri
  2. Make the Stew
  3. Make the Ewedu
  4. Make the Amala
  5. Serve in a dip bowl or multiple plates
